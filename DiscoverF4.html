Diniguan

Dinuguan is a Filipino savory stew made primarily of pork meat and offal, such as intestines and liver, cooked in a rich and thick sauce made from pig's blood, vinegar, and various spices including garlic, onions, and chili peppers. It is often served with rice and is known for its bold and distinctive flavor profile, combining savory, sour, and spicy elements. Dinuguan is a popular dish in Filipino cuisine, enjoyed by many for its unique taste and cultural significance.
